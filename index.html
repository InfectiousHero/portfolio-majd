01 — About

Hello, I'm
Majd Al Khori.

I'm a Computer Science student at the University of Ottawa with a genuine interest in how people interact with technology — and how good design can make those interactions feel simple and natural.

Off-screen you'll find me on the football pitch — playing or watching. The team strategy and quick decision-making in football actually influence how I think about design: clarity under pressure and always reading the situation. I also enjoy the calm focus of billiards, where every angle matters.

My faith as a Christian shapes how I see the world. I believe in building things that are honest, purposeful, and human — and that same principle guides my approach to design.

02 — Process

How I
Work.

As a student in SEG3125 at the University of Ottawa, I'm actively building my design process. Like football — where reading the game matters as much as the technique — good design starts with understanding the situation first.

Resources

Nielsen Norman Group (NN/g) Interaction Design Foundation Bootstrap 5 — W3Schools SEG3125 — University of Ottawa
01

Understand & Research

Like studying a team before a match — I start by understanding user needs and goals before touching any design element.

02

Define & Ideate

Translating research into clear problems. I sketch multiple directions before committing — like planning plays before choosing one.

03

Prototype & Build

From wireframes to HTML/CSS/JS with Bootstrap 5 and React. Good design must be buildable, so I test real interactions early.

04

Evaluate & Iterate

Using heuristic evaluation and peer feedback to improve. Every good result — in design or in sport — comes from honest review.
